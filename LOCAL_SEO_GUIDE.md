### Step 1: Create or Claim Your GBP Listing

1. Go to [business.google.com](https://business.google.com)
2. Sign in with the Google account you want to manage the business with (use a dedicated business Gmail if possible, not your personal one).
3. Click **"Add your business to Google"**
4. Type **"Teflon Don LLC"** -- if it already exists, click it and claim it. If not, click **"Add your business"**.
5. Select your business category (see below).
6. Choose **"Yes"** when asked if you want to add a location customers can visit.
   - If you work from a home office and do NOT want the address public, choose **"No, I deliver goods and services to my customers"** and set a **service area** instead.

- **Phone verification** -- They call or text (518) 698-9870 with a code.
- **Postcard verification** -- Takes 5-14 days. A postcard arrives at your business address with a PIN.
- **Email verification** -- Sometimes available. Check if dismail93@gmail.com is offered.

## 8. Competitor Analysis Framework

### How to Research Houston Web Development Competitors

Understanding your competition is not about copying them -- it is about finding gaps they are missing and exploiting them.

### Step 1: Identify Your Top Competitors

**Search these exact phrases on Google and note who appears in positions 1-10 and in the Maps 3-pack:**

```
web designer houston
web design houston tx
website developer houston
houston web design company
seo company houston
seo services houston tx
web design [neighborhood] houston (for each target neighborhood)
```

**Create a spreadsheet with these columns:**

| Column | What to Track |
|---|---|
| Business Name | Exact name as shown on Google |
| Website URL | Their website address |
| GBP Link | Link to their Google Business Profile |
| Maps Ranking | Where they appear in the 3-pack (1st, 2nd, 3rd, or not shown) |
| Organic Ranking | Position in organic results |
| Review Count | Number of Google reviews |
| Review Rating | Average star rating |
| Primary GBP Category | What is their primary category |
| Estimated Monthly Traffic | Check with Ubersuggest or SimilarWeb (free versions) |
| Backlink Count | Check with Ahrefs free backlink checker or Ubersuggest |
| Years in Business | From their GBP "years in business" badge |
| Services Offered | What services they list |
| Pricing | If visible on their site |
| Weaknesses | What they are doing poorly |

### Step 2: Analyze Their Google Business Profiles

For each competitor in the top 10, check:

- [ ] How many reviews do they have? (Your target: beat the average)
- [ ] How recent is their latest review? (If stale, you can outpace them)
- [ ] What categories are they using? (Copy the good ones, add ones they missed)
- [ ] How many photos do they have? (Your target: have more)
- [ ] Are they posting regularly? (If not, this is your advantage)
- [ ] What shows up in their Q&A? (Pre-populate yours better)
- [ ] What services are listed? (Match or exceed)

### Step 3: Analyze Their Websites

For each competitor's website, evaluate:

**Technical SEO:**
- Run their site through PageSpeed Insights (pagespeed.web.dev) -- note their score
- Check if they are mobile-responsive
- Check their Core Web Vitals scores
- Note their site speed (if yours is faster, that is a ranking advantage)

**Content Analysis:**
- How many pages do they have? (Check with `site:theirwebsite.com` on Google)
- Do they have location pages? (e.g., `/web-design-katy/`)
- Do they have a blog? How often do they post?
- What topics are they covering?
- What topics are they NOT covering? (This is your opportunity)

**Conversion Analysis:**
- How clear is their CTA (call to action)?
- Do they show pricing?
- Do they have case studies or portfolio?
- Do they have testimonials?
- How easy is it to contact them?

### Step 4: Find Content Gaps

Use this process:

1. Go to Ubersuggest (neilpatel.com/ubersuggest) -- free version works
2. Enter each competitor's domain
3. Look at their "Top Pages" and "Keywords" reports
4. Note keywords they rank for that you do NOT have content for
5. Create content targeting those keywords

**Common gaps in the Houston market:**
- Most Houston web designers do NOT have neighborhood-specific pages
- Most do NOT have industry-specific content (e.g., "HVAC website design Houston")
- Most do NOT blog consistently
- Most do NOT have AI automation as a service (this is your differentiator)
- Most do NOT respond to reviews or post on GBP regularly

### Step 5: Competitive Advantages to Exploit

Based on typical Houston web design market gaps, here are your likely advantages:

| Your Advantage | How to Exploit It |
|---|---|
| **AI Automation** | No one else is leading with this. Make it your #1 differentiator. |
| **GBP Posting** | Most competitors post rarely or never. Post 2-3x/week and you dominate. |
| **Location Pages** | Create pages for 10+ Houston suburbs. Most competitors have zero. |
| **Blog Content** | Publish 2-4 posts/month. Most competitors publish 0-1. |
| **Review Velocity** | Actively generate reviews monthly. Most competitors do not ask. |
| **Personal Brand** | "Donny from Teflon Don" is more memorable than "Generic Web Agency." Lead with personality. |
| **Speed** | Promise and deliver faster turnaround than larger agencies. |
| **Niche Focus** | Target specific industries (HVAC, dental, restaurants) instead of "everyone." |

### Monthly Competitor Check-In

**Every month, spend 30 minutes doing this:**

1. Search your top 5 keywords -- note any ranking changes
2. Check competitor review counts -- are they growing faster than you?
3. Look at competitor GBP posts -- are they stepping up their game?
4. Check if any new competitors have appeared in the Maps pack
5. Review competitor websites for new content or features
6. Adjust your strategy based on findings
