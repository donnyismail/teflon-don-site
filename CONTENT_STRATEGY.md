#### Week 1: The Audit Bomb

**Title:** "I Audited 50 Houston Business Websites. Here's What I Found."

**Target Keyword:** Houston business website audit
**Secondary Keywords:** Houston website problems, Houston small business website review
**Estimated Monthly Search Volume:** 210 (low competition, high intent)

**Outline:**

1. **Hook** -- "I spent 3 weeks pulling up the websites of 50 Houston businesses -- restaurants, plumbers, law firms, boutiques. The results were brutal."
2. **Methodology** -- How the audit was conducted (PageSpeed Insights, mobile testing, SEO crawl, conversion analysis). Name the tools: GTmetrix, Screaming Frog, Google Lighthouse.
3. **Finding 1: Speed** -- 72% of sites took longer than 4 seconds to load. Include Houston-specific data (average mobile connection speeds in Third Ward vs. The Woodlands).
4. **Finding 2: Mobile** -- 58% had broken mobile layouts. Screenshot examples (anonymized or with permission).
5. **Finding 3: SEO** -- 81% had no meta descriptions, no local schema markup, and zero Google Business Profile optimization.
6. **Finding 4: Conversion** -- 90% had no clear call-to-action above the fold. No phone number. No form. No chat widget.
7. **The Cost** -- Back-of-napkin math: if you get 500 visitors/month and your conversion rate drops from 3% to 0.5% because of a bad site, that's 12-13 lost customers/month. At an average ticket of $200, that's $2,500/month walking out the door.
8. **What the Top 10% Did Right** -- The handful of sites that were actually good. What separated them.
9. **CTA** -- "Want to know where your website falls? I'll audit your site for free. No pitch, just a screen-recorded video walkthrough showing you exactly what's broken. [Book Your Free Audit]"

**Internal Links:** Link to pillar page on Houston web design (once published).
**Content Upgrade:** Downloadable "Houston Website Scorecard" (PDF checklist).

## 5. Case Study Template

### The Case Study Framework: Challenge > Solution > Results

#### Section 1: The Snapshot

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Client Name** | [Business name -- with permission, or anonymized as "A Houston [industry] business"] |
| **Industry** | [e.g., Plumbing, Restaurant, Law Firm, Retail] |
| **Location** | [Houston neighborhood or suburb, e.g., Katy, TX] |
| **Service Provided** | [Web Design, SEO, GBP Optimization, Full Package] |
| **Timeline** | [e.g., 4 weeks for build, 90 days for results] |
| **Investment** | [Price range or exact, with client permission] |

---

#### Section 2: The Challenge

Write this as a story, not a spec sheet. Make the reader see themselves in the client's situation.

**Prompts to answer:**
- What was the client's business situation before working with Teflon Don?
- What specific problems were they experiencing? (Low traffic, no leads, bad website, invisible on Google, losing to competitors)
- How long had the problem existed?
- What had they tried before? (DIY, another agency, ignoring it)
- What was the financial impact of the problem? (Lost revenue, wasted ad spend, missed opportunities)
- What was the emotional state? (Frustrated, overwhelmed, skeptical, desperate)

**Example:**
> "Marcus had been running his plumbing business in Katy for 11 years. Word-of-mouth had always been enough -- until it wasn't. New competitors were popping up with professional websites and strong Google rankings. Marcus's website was a single page he'd built on Wix in 2019. It didn't rank for anything. His phone went from ringing 10 times a week to 2. He was considering taking on a partner just to cover overhead."

---

#### Section 3: The Solution

Walk through exactly what was done, step by step. Be specific enough to demonstrate expertise but accessible enough for a non-technical reader.

**Prompts to answer:**
- What was the strategic approach? (Not just "we built a website" but "we conducted a competitive audit and identified 15 high-intent keywords that competitors were ranking for")
- What was the design/build process?
- What specific features were implemented and why?
- What SEO strategy was executed?
- What tools or technologies were used?
- How was the client involved in the process?
- What was the timeline from kickoff to launch?

**Structure the solution in phases:**
1. **Discovery & Audit** -- What we found, what the competitive landscape looked like
2. **Strategy** -- The game plan, keyword targets, content strategy, technical priorities
3. **Design & Build** -- The website itself, key features, UX decisions
4. **Optimization** -- SEO implementation, GBP setup, speed optimization
5. **Launch & Iterate** -- Go-live, monitoring, adjustments

---

#### Section 4: The Results

This is the money section. Lead with numbers. Be specific. Be honest.

**Metrics to Capture (track ALL of these from Day 1):**

**Website Performance:**
- Page load speed (before/after)
- Mobile responsiveness score (before/after)
- Google Lighthouse score (before/after)
- Bounce rate (before/after)

**Traffic:**
- Monthly organic visitors (before/after, tracked monthly)
- Traffic sources breakdown (organic, direct, referral, social)
- Top landing pages

**Search Rankings:**
- Google ranking for primary target keywords (before/after)
- Number of first-page rankings
- Google Maps/Local Pack visibility
- Google Business Profile metrics (views, searches, actions)

**Leads & Revenue:**
- Inbound calls per week/month (before/after)
- Form submissions per week/month (before/after)
- Email inquiries per week/month (before/after)
- Estimated revenue impact (leads x average ticket value)
- Cost per lead (if applicable)
- ROI calculation (revenue generated / investment)

**Reviews & Reputation:**
- Google review count (before/after)
- Average star rating (before/after)
- Review response rate

**Example Results Block:**

> **30-Day Results:**
> - Organic traffic: 127/month --> 843/month (+564%)
> - Google ranking for "plumber Katy TX": Not ranked --> #3
